## 🧠 Philippe Laffont — Founder & CIO, Coatue Management

### Background
- CS degree from MIT; describes himself as a "repressed mediocre computer scientist" — he thinks like an engineer
- Founded Coatue in 1999 after leaving Julian Robertson's Tiger Management (Tiger Cub lineage)
- ~$36–41B AUM across public equities and private venture; invested in Anthropic
- Personal net worth ~$6.5B; checks Bloomberg at 3am nightly — obsessively data-driven

### Investment Philosophy
- **Growth investor** focused on revenue/earnings inflections and technology regime changes
- Famously mapped the history of computing dominance: IBM mainframes (1960–1980) → PC era → Internet → Mobile → **AI (now)**
- His "token economy" thesis: intelligence itself will become a priced, tradeable commodity
- Studies *30 bubbles over 400 years* before investing — thinks in historical cycles, not quarters
- Tracks AI adoption via *real behavioral data* (e.g., Coatue was first to link ChatGPT signups to declining Google page views)
- Concept of "Coatue Fantastic 40" — forward-looking list of 40 top growth opportunities for 5-year horizon
- Invests in **AI physical layer**: power grids, chip fabs, cooling systems, data center land — "infrastructure before apps"
- Coined: "We're moving to the token economy" — AI as a utility, not a novelty

### Current Portfolio Signals (as of early 2026)
- Top AI holdings: Meta, Microsoft, Amazon, CoreWeave, Taiwan Semiconductor, Synopsys, Applied Materials
- Trimmed Alphabet (linked it to AI-driven search displacement), then added it back to Fantastic 40
- Invested directly in Anthropic (private)
- Bullish on infrastructure enablers, not just AI app companies

### What Will Resonate With Laffont
- **Historical framing matters** — he loves mapping tech waves; a pitch that connects computing history to AI's current moment will land hard
- **Behavioral data over consensus** — find a non-obvious signal (like his ChatGPT/Google page view insight); this is his highest-value intellectual move
- **Physical layer thinking** — companies solving problems in compute efficiency, memory, training data quality, or inference optimization fit his worldview
- **The "token economy" lens** — frame the company as critical infrastructure for pricing and distributing AI intelligence
- **Rigorous financial modeling** — he's an engineer at heart; sloppy numbers are disqualifying
- Avoid: vague "AI will be big" narratives; pure software plays without defensible moats; consensus trades

## 🗡️ Dan Loeb — Founder & CEO, Third Point

### Background
- BA from Columbia; grew up in Santa Monica; surfer turned Wall Street's sharpest pen
- Founded Third Point in 1995; ~$24B AUM; annualized ~16% since inception (roughly 3x S&P)
- Famous for "poison pen" letters to management teams — surgical, biting, often devastating
- Sits on boards: US Olympic Committee, LACMA, Council on Foreign Relations
- Philanthropist: Alzheimer's Drug Discovery Foundation, education reform, LGBT rights

### Investment Philosophy
- **Activist-event driven** — hunts for companies in the middle of shake-ups, transitions, or with fixable capital allocation failures
- High conviction, concentrated: top 10 holdings = 50%+ of portfolio; 58%+ annual turnover
- Rotates aggressively into perceived mispricings across sectors (not a pure-tech investor)
- **Q4 2025 thesis:** AI is *destroying the moat* of capital-light software/info services businesses; rotating into capital-intensive companies building real infrastructure
- Warning quote (Q4 2025 letter): Anthropic's announcements were *"dislocating businesses that provide legal and financial analysis"* — he sees AI disruption of white-collar work as already underway
- Seeks undervalued international AI plays (SK Hynix, Ebara) where US markets haven't yet priced in AI tailwinds
- Loves companies with activist catalysts: new management, board changes, capital allocation overhaul

### Current Portfolio Signals (as of early 2026)
- Big adds: Microsoft (+175%), Nvidia, SK Hynix (activist position)
- New positions: CoStar (activist), Chipotle, Spotify, Alibaba
- Top 2025 contributors: Siemens Energy, US Steel, Nvidia, Taiwan Semiconductor, SK Hynix
- Trimmed: Alphabet, Meta (took losses), Capital One
- Key theme: semiconductors/memory > software; European defense; infrastructure beats SaaS

### What Will Resonate With Loeb
- **Specific catalyst** — what event, announcement, or structural shift is the trigger? Loeb doesn't buy "eventually good" companies; he buys misunderstood companies on the edge of a re-rating
- **Moat analysis with intellectual honesty** — he explicitly warned that AI is eroding software moats; show you understand which moats are real vs. illusory
- **Data-driven demolition of consensus** — don't tell him what everyone already knows; show him the thing the market is mispricing
- **Valuation discipline** — he cares deeply about price paid; include a clear bull/base/bear framework with specific numbers
- **Capital allocation scrutiny** — if the company has a capital misallocation story (or a management team solving one), that's activist candy
- His aesthetic: sharp, direct, no hedging, zero tolerance for MBA-speak or hand-waving
- Avoid: pure growth-at-any-price narratives; consensus "AI will be huge" takes; companies with bad management that can't be fixed

## 🏔️ Ken Hao — Chairman & Managing Partner, Silver Lake

### Background
- AB in Economics from Harvard (tennis team); former Managing Director at Hambrecht & Quist (now JPMorgan)
- Joined Silver Lake in 2000; established Asian offices in 2007–08; led investments in Alibaba Group and Ant Financial
- Board seats: Broadcom, Altera (Silver Lake's $8.75B FPGA acquisition from Intel, 2025)
- Trustee: Metropolitan Museum of Art, UCSF Foundation, UTR Sports
- Long-term operator-oriented investor; not a trader — holds companies, transforms them

### Investment Philosophy
- Silver Lake's core thesis: **large-cap, mature technology companies with transformable capital structures**
- Not a startup VC — invests in *proven* companies at the intersection of technology and scale
- Deep expertise in semiconductors, software infrastructure, enterprise tech, and Asia tech
- The Altera deal (2025) is the defining signal: bet on **programmable silicon as AI's flexible compute layer** — FPGAs for edge AI, robotics, data centers
- Quote on Altera: *"Once-in-a-generation opportunity to invest in a scale leader in advanced semiconductors... investing in emerging AI-driven markets such as edge computing and robotics"*
- Strongly believes in technology's role in art, culture, and society (Met trustee, UCSF)
- Asia-focused experience: understands supply chain, semiconductor geopolitics, and cross-border tech dynamics

### Current Portfolio Signals (as of early 2026)
- Altera (FPGA leader, AI edge computing, robotics): defining bet
- $400M data center land joint venture: AI infrastructure land banking
- Broadcom board: deep semiconductor/networking exposure
- Historical: Alibaba, Ant Financial, Splunk, SolarWinds, Symantec, Avago (became Broadcom)

### What Will Resonate With Hao
- **Semiconductor and programmable compute angles** — he has deep conviction here; FPGAs, edge AI, inference silicon are his territory
- **Scale and defensibility** — Silver Lake doesn't do moonshots; they do large, defensible, transformable businesses
- **AI infrastructure as physical/real asset** — data center land, compute capacity, power — he's actively building this portfolio
- **Asian technology angle** — supply chain, manufacturing, cross-border IP dynamics (but not just geopolitical noise)
- **Long holding period thinking** — he doesn't flip; show why the company is stronger in 5-10 years
- **Operational rigor** — Silver Lake works with management teams; a pitch that includes operational transformation levers (not just market narrative) is more credible
- **Art/culture intersection** — while less directly relevant, he appreciates elegance in presentation and thinking
- Avoid: pure software/SaaS with no defensible IP; early-stage moonshots without revenue; companies without clear paths to operational scale

### The Unified Judge Narrative:
All three judges are converging on the same macro bet: **AI needs to be built, not just used.** The physical and intellectual infrastructure — chips, compute, data quality, training efficiency, inference optimization — is where the next decade of value creation lives. A pitch in this space, with a non-consensus angle and rigorous numbers, is speaking directly to the shared thesis of all three.

The pitch should feel like it was written by someone who thinks in *paradigm shifts* (Laffont), has *surgical precision* on valuation and management (Loeb), and understands *scale, defensibility, and operational transformation* (Hao).
