# Stage 01 — Civilizational History & Paradigm Shifts

**Perplexity Task:** Task 1  
**Purpose:** Build the macro historical argument that grounds the entire investment thesis

---

## What This Stage Produced

Research and data establishing that every major technology paradigm shift — Digital (1950s–1990s), Internet (1993–2010s), AI (2020s–) — followed the same structural pattern: application-layer companies get priced up first, infrastructure companies compound the most over the long run. This is the foundational argument for why we are looking at AI infrastructure, not AI apps.

### Key Arguments Built Here

- **The infrastructure immortals pattern:** Cisco, Oracle, and eventually AWS dominated Internet-era infrastructure while thousands of application startups failed. The AI equivalents are being built now and are still mispriced.
- **The token economy frame (Laffont's language):** Intelligence becoming a priced, tradeable commodity — like electricity, like bandwidth. Whoever owns the pipes captures durable value.
- **Quantified productivity waves:** GDP-per-hour productivity data across each paradigm shift, sourced from Statista and historical economic databases.
- **The lightbulb analogy:** AI doesn't replace human thought — it restructures civilization's relationship with knowledge, scale, and specialization.
