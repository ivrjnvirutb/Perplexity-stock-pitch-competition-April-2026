Perplexity's stock pitch competition is judged by Philippe Laffont (Founder & CIO at Coatue Management), Daniel Loeb (Founder & CEO at Third Point LLC), and Kenneth Hao (Chairman and Managing Partner at Silver Lake).

3 of the most respected names in institutional investing.

i'm not a financial analyst.
i don't have a bloomberg terminal.
i don't have a team.



here's what i've realized about where we actually are:

AI is not a productivity tool. it's a paradigm shift — the same category as electricity and the internet.

and like every paradigm shift before it, the people who adapt first won't be the ones who had the most resources.

they'll be the ones who understood what changed.



what changed is this:

execution is nearly free now.

for individuals, that means the gap between having an idea and doing something real with it has collapsed. a student on spring break can produce work that competes with institutional research teams. not because AI thinks for you — but because it removes the friction between your thinking and its output.

for businesses, that means the competitive advantage is no longer locked in headcount, capital, or proprietary tools. it's locked in judgment. in the ability to frame the right problem, ask the right question, and recognize a good answer when you see one.

that's not a small shift. that's a reorganization of who gets to participate.



but here's the part that gets lost in the AI conversation:

the tools only work if you bring something to them.

i didn't open Perplexity and ask it to write me a stock pitch.

i spent time understanding the platform's capabilities before designing a single task. i profiled the judges — their frameworks, what they find lazy, what earns their respect. i built a thesis grounded in civilizational history before touching a single company. i defined what the final output needed to look like before i wrote the first prompt.

the AI executed. the thinking was mine.

that's the distinction that matters — and it's the one most people are getting wrong.

using AI to skip the thinking is how you produce average outputs at scale. using AI to extend your thinking is how you produce something that couldn't have existed before.



the bottleneck in this new world isn't access to tools.

it's the quality of human judgment behind them.

intuition. taste. the ability to feel when something is off before you can articulate why. the willingness to sit with a problem long enough to develop a real point of view before handing it to a machine.

those things can't be prompted. they have to be earned.

and that's what makes this moment more hopeful than it is frightening.

AI doesn't flatten human potential. it amplifies it — for anyone willing to keep thinking.



soft skills aren't soft anymore.

they're the hardest thing left to replicate.

and i think that's the most exciting sentence i've ever gotten to write.
