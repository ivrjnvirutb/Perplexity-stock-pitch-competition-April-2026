## App Architecture: Eight Chapters

The app tells a story — earning the answer, not opening with it:

| Chapter | Title | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | The World Before the Answer | Civilizational history of tech paradigm shifts |
| 2 | Locating the Opportunity | Full AI infrastructure universe, 5 risk/reward tiers |
| 3 | The Comparative Analysis | Head-to-head finalists → selection rationale |
| 4 | The Company | CRDO deep dive: moat, financials, alt data signals |
| 5 | The Financial Case | DCF scenarios, comps, sensitivity, catalyst calendar |
| 6 | The Stress Test | Model Council: 3 models × 5 adversarial questions |
| 7 | The Recommendation | Final thesis, conviction pillars per judge, risks |
| 8 | How This Was Built | Full Perplexity Computer process log and methodology |

---

## Design Principles

**Narrative over navigation:** Users move through the story sequentially. The stock is not revealed until Chapter 4 — judges are brought through the full argument before the answer is given.

**Methodology is visible:** Chapter 8 ("How This Was Built") documents every sub-agent, every premium data source, every Model Council query. The creative use of Perplexity Computer is not just implied — it is a dedicated chapter.

**Judge calibration:** The app surfaces judge-specific conviction pillars in Chapter 7:
- Laffont: Infrastructure-before-apps, token economy, behavioral data signals
- Loeb: Catalyst timing, valuation discipline, non-consensus evidence
- Hao: Semiconductor defensibility, 5–10 year compounding, operational scale
