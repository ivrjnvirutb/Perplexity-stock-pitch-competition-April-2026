Critical Divergences — Shown Explicitly

Durable Moat at 5 Years?

GPT-5.4: YES (narrower, execution-dependent) Opus 4.6: NO (degrades at 448G) Gemini: NO (hyperscalers will fund alternatives)

Two of three models concluded the moat degrades at 448G. Our view: the 24–30 month head start wins at 224G regardless. Evidence that would change this: Marvell in broad production at a second hyperscaler before June 2026.

Bear Case Split — Two Distinct Paths

GPT-5.4 + Opus 4.6: Marvell qualification → multiple compression Gemini (UNIQUE): Optical substitution — LPO/CPO replaces copper AEC

Gemini's bear case is orthogonal to Marvell — it's not competition within AEC, it's obsolescence of the AEC category. At 10–15% probability weight, this scenario reduces the bull-case ceiling even in a CRDO-wins-vs-Marvell world.

Synthesis: What All Three Models Agreed On
  • The "mandatory IP toll gate" framing is overstated — ITC settlements with Molex and TE Connectivity were cable assembler agreements, not blocking patents against semiconductor competitors.
  • Customer concentration is the paramount near-term risk — one customer at ~67% of FY2025 revenue creates fragility disproportionate to the thesis's confidence level.
  • Qualification lead-time is real but not insurmountable — especially for Marvell, who has existing hyperscaler relationships in switching and DPUs that compress cold-start disadvantage.
  • The 448G / 200G-per-lane transition is the key competitive inflection point — Marvell's DesignCon 2026 demo is a concrete signal that the competitive window at next-gen is real.
  • Second hyperscaler achieving broad production deployment with Credo AECs is the single most conviction-enhancing data point any model identified.
Three Thesis Adjustments Made Post-Council
  • Language reframe: "Mandatory IP toll gate" → "Qualified incumbent with strong but time-limited first-mover advantage." More defensible, more accurate.
  • Optical substitution scenario added: 10–15% probability weight on LPO/CPO achieving cost/power parity at 3–7m within 36 months. Caps the AEC TAM below $5B base case in that scenario.
  • Multiple compression sensitivity added: Opus 4.6 quantified: 10x → 5-6x forward revenue re-rating = $55–70 stock price even with strong revenue execution. Proactively disclosed in risk section.
The Dan Loeb Question
If you were Dan Loeb on an earnings call with Credo's CEO:
"Bill, you've described full swim-lane penetration at your largest hyperscaler and engagement with five of the top seven. Marvell demonstrated a working 224G 1.6T AEC at DesignCon in February with 10⁻¹⁰ pre-FEC BER over 3 meters on their Alaska A DSP. My question is specific: Of your top three hyperscaler customers, how many have formally indicated they will NOT qualify a second AEC source for their next-generation 1.6T rack architecture — and for those that are evaluating alternatives, at what stage of qualification is the competing solution today? Management's answer to prepare:
"We do not expect any hyperscaler to commit publicly or contractually to never qualifying a second AEC source — that would be inconsistent with standard procurement practice for any strategic component. We've never built our business plan around permanent sole-source status, and our investors shouldn't either. What we can tell you is this: the competitive dynamics favor the qualified incumbent far more than the challenger. On Marvell's DesignCon demo — a working demo at a trade show and a qualified, production-validated AEC deployed at hyperscale are separated by 18–24 months of reliability testing, thermal characterization, signal-integrity validation across real-world cable lengths and topologies, firmware maturity, and system-level integration work. We went through this process ourselves. Qualifying a new AEC supplier means re-validating every swim lane: GPU-to-host, NIC-to-ToR, and disaggregated switch fabric. We are not standing still at 100G. We have 200G-per-lane product sampling today, PCIe 6.0 AECs targeting mass production in the first half of fiscal 2027, and full roadmap alignment with both Ethernet and UALink for 1.6T."
